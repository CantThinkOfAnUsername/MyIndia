Sandesh (Bengali: সন্দেশ Shôndesh) a dessert, originating from the Bengal region in the eastern part of the Indian subcontinent, created with milk and sugar.[1] Some recipes of Sandesh call for the use of chhena or paneer nstead of milk itself.
Rosogolla or Rasagola or Rasgulla is a Bengali syrupy dessert popular all over India. It is made from ball-shaped dumplings of chhena (an Indian cottage cheese) and semolina dough, cooked in light syrup made of sugar.
